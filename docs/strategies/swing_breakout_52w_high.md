# 52-Week-High Breakout Swing

## What it is
The 52-week-high breakout is a classical price-action setup popularized by
William O'Neil (CAN SLIM) and many trend-following traders. Buy a stock as it
breaks decisively above its 52-week high on above-average volume, with a hard
stop typically 5-8% below entry. Ride the trend for 4-12 weeks, exit on a
violation of a near-term moving average or the stop. Position sizing is
risk-based: never lose more than ~1% of account on a single trade.

## When it works
- **Trending bull regimes**: 2020 H2, 2023 — fresh highs lead to more highs.
- **Strong relative strength**: best when the stock outperforms its sector.
- **Earnings-fueled breakouts**: post-earnings highs on raised guidance have
  the best follow-through (PEAD overlap).

## When it fails
- **Choppy markets**: 2015, mid-2022 — repeated stop-outs.
- **False breakouts**: ~50-60% of breakouts fail within 5 days. Disciplined
  exits are mandatory.
- **Gap-down risk**: news after the close can blow through stops.
- **PDT trap**: at $10k, exit-then-re-enter on a single day = 1 round trip.
  Three of those in 5 days = PDT flag.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- All gains are short-term (held < 12 months) → ordinary-income rates.
- **Wash-sale rule**: a stopped-out position re-entered within 30 days
  triggers wash-sale, deferring the loss. Common with this strategy. IRS
  Pub 550 §1091 governs.
- Track every round trip carefully — 1099-B reports wash-sale adjustments
  but only across like accounts.

## $10k feasibility
Feasible but tight. $10k can run 4-5 concurrent positions of $2,000 each.
Stop discipline (1% of account = $100 per trade) limits position size such
that you may be using more than 1% per trade with this little capital.
Consider running 2-3 trades concurrently instead.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Build a watchlist of 30 large-cap leaders (top 30 by relative strength
   in the S&P 500 over 26 weeks).
2. Each evening, scan for names closing within 1% of their 52-week high on
   >150% of average volume.
3. Enter next morning at first 5-minute high. Set hard stop 7% below.
4. Log every trade: entry, stop, exit, reason. Review weekly.
5. Track day-trade count to stay PDT-safe (max 3 round trips per 5 trading
   days).
