# Bogleheads Three-Fund Portfolio

## What it is
The Three-Fund Portfolio is the canonical Bogleheads "lazy portfolio": one
US total-market ETF (VTI), one international total-market ETF (VXUS), and one
US total-bond ETF (BND). A typical allocation for someone in their 30s is
60% VTI / 30% VXUS / 10% BND, shifting more bond as you age. Annual or
threshold-based rebalancing keeps you on target. Total expense ratio under
0.05%, no manager risk, broad global diversification.

## When it works
- **Long-run compounding**: matches global market returns minus minimal fees.
- **Predictable rebalancing dividend**: selling winners and buying losers
  systematically captures mean reversion.
- **Tax-efficient with care**: hold bonds in IRA, equities in taxable for
  best tax placement.

## When it fails
- **Decade of US-only outperformance**: 2010-2020 international lagged
  badly; the three-fund mix underperformed an all-VTI portfolio by 3-4%/yr.
- **2022 correlation breakdown**: stocks AND bonds fell. The diversification
  premise broke for ~14 months.
- **Behavioral**: rebalancing requires SELLING winners — many investors won't.
- **Tax drag in taxable**: bond fund interest is ordinary-income; rebalancing
  triggers gains.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- VTI and VXUS qualified dividends → LTCG rates if holding-period met.
- BND distributions are ordinary income — best held in IRA.
- VXUS pays through foreign tax credits (Form 1116), usable only in taxable
  accounts. Holding VXUS in IRA wastes the FTC.
- Rebalancing in taxable account triggers gains — prefer rebalancing with new
  contributions.
- See IRS Pub 550 for cost-basis methods.

## $10k feasibility
Cleanly feasible. $10k splits across 3 ETFs without rounding issues at
brokers offering fractional shares (Fidelity, Schwab).

## First-week starter checklist
1. Pick allocation. Default for 30s: 60/30/10 VTI/VXUS/BND.
2. If most of your savings are in IRA, place BND in IRA, VTI/VXUS wherever.
3. Buy this week — lump sum or 4-week DCA.
4. Enable DRIP on all three.
5. Set quarterly calendar reminder: rebalance only via new cash, never sell.
