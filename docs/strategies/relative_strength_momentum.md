# Cross-Sectional Momentum (Top-Decile)

## What it is
Cross-sectional momentum buys stocks (or ETFs) that have outperformed the
universe over the past 6-12 months and shorts (or avoids) the laggards.
Documented in academic finance since Jegadeesh & Titman (1993). The standard
formulation skips the most recent month to avoid short-term reversal: rank by
"12-month minus 1-month" return ("12-1 momentum"). Long-only retail version:
buy the top decile of S&P 500 ranked by 12-1 momentum, equal-weight, rebalance
monthly. Or skip the DIY work entirely and buy iShares MTUM.

## When it works
- **Trending markets**: 2017, 2020 H2, 2023 — strong momentum factor returns.
- **Persistent leaders**: when a leader (e.g., NVDA in 2023-2024) compounds
  for many months, momentum captures the bulk of it.
- **Low-correlation regime**: when winners and losers diverge widely.

## When it fails
- **Momentum crashes after deep bear-market bottoms**: April 2009 saw
  momentum lose ~30% in 6 months as junk-rallied off the bottom outpaced
  prior winners. Same dynamic March 2020 → June 2020.
- **Choppy markets**: 2015-2016 momentum churned with monthly whipsaws.
- **High turnover**: monthly rebalances trigger heavy short-term capital
  gains in taxable accounts.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- DIY monthly rebalances → almost all gains short-term (ordinary income).
- Wash-sale risk is real: a stock dropped from the basket at a loss this
  month, re-added next month, triggers wash-sale (IRS Pub 550). Hard to
  avoid with high-turnover momentum.
- ETF wrapper (MTUM) handles all rebalancing inside the fund, deferring
  taxation until you sell — large advantage.

## $10k feasibility
- **MTUM**: trivial — buy any dollar amount. Recommended for $10k-level
  capital.
- **DIY 10-name long basket**: feasible with $1,000 per name, but 24+ round
  trips/year breaches PDT if executed clumsily. At $10k, prefer the ETF.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Decision: ETF route (MTUM, easy, tax-efficient) or DIY (more control,
   more taxes).
2. ETF: buy $1,500-3,000 MTUM. Hold 12+ months minimum to capture the LTCG
   rate.
3. DIY: download S&P 500 list, compute 12-1 returns, take top 10, equal
   weight. Rebalance the first trading day of each month.
4. Track regime: if S&P 200-DMA is rising, run; if falling, consider going
   to cash (defensive overlay reduces crash risk).
