# Quality Factor ETF (QUAL / SPHQ)

## What it is
The "quality" factor identifies companies with high return on equity, low
debt-to-equity, and stable earnings growth. Theoretical foundation in Asness
et al.'s "Quality Minus Junk" (2013). Implementations: iShares QUAL (MSCI USA
Sector Neutral Quality, ~0.15% ER) and Invesco SPHQ (S&P 500 Quality Index,
~0.15% ER). Comparable long-term returns to S&P 500 with lower drawdowns —
a "smoother ride" sleeve for long-term investors.

## When it works
- **Late-cycle environments**: high-quality balance sheets matter when
  funding costs rise.
- **Recessionary periods**: quality stocks weather earnings declines better.
- **Compounding focus**: quality companies historically reinvest at high ROIC.

## When it fails
- **Junk rallies after deep selloffs**: April 2009 saw low-quality stocks
  rip; quality lagged.
- **High correlation to S&P 500**: in normal regimes, QUAL behaves a lot
  like the index. Limited diversification.
- **Definition drift**: each provider's "quality" definition differs — read
  the methodology.
- **Tech-heavy in some implementations**: QUAL has substantial Apple/Microsoft
  exposure, reducing the diversification benefit.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Standard equity ETF treatment. Hold >12 months for LTCG.
- Qualified dividends → LTCG rates if holding-period rule met (IRS Pub 550).
- Low wash-sale risk for buy-and-hold; rises if tax-loss harvesting between
  similar quality ETFs.

## $10k feasibility
Trivial. $1,000-1,500 satellite allocation works.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Pick QUAL or SPHQ — they target slightly different baskets but overlap
   significantly.
2. Buy $1,500 as a defensive equity sleeve alongside VTI core.
3. Hold for at least 5 years before judging.
4. Read the index methodology document to understand inclusion criteria.
