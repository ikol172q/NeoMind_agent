# Permanent Portfolio (Harry Browne)

## What it is
Designed by Harry Browne in 1981, the Permanent Portfolio holds 25% each of
US equities (VTI), long-term Treasuries (TLT), gold (GLD), and cash/short
T-bills (SHY or BIL). The premise: each asset thrives in one macro regime —
stocks in prosperity, long bonds in deflation, gold in inflation, cash in
recession. Annual rebalancing back to 25/25/25/25 forces selling whichever
quadrant just ran.

## When it works
- **Crisis resilience**: max drawdowns historically 15-20% vs ~50% for 100%
  equity. Sleeps well at night.
- **Inflation regime (1970s, 2022 H1)**: gold and short cash protect.
- **Deflation/recession**: long bonds fly (2008 was a banner year for the PP).
- **Behavioral simplicity**: one rebalance per year, no decisions.

## When it fails
- **Long bull markets in equities**: 2010-2020 PP returned ~6%/yr vs S&P
  ~13%/yr. The opportunity cost is severe.
- **2022 correlation crisis**: stocks AND bonds AND gold all fell. Only cash
  held up. The "four uncorrelated assets" premise broke.
- **Real-rate spikes**: TLT can lose 30%+ in a year (2022 actual).
- **Gold lost decade**: 2012-2018 gold went sideways, dragging the PP.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- **GLD is taxed as a collectible** under IRC §408(m) — long-term gains rate
  is **28%**, not 20%. This is a frequently-missed gotcha. (See IRS Pub 550.)
- Alternatives: IAU has the same issue; physical-gold-backed ETFs are all
  collectibles. PHYS (Sprott) elects PFIC treatment, which can be even worse.
- TLT distributions: ordinary-income interest, not qualified dividends.
- Best held in tax-advantaged account if possible.
- Wash-sale risk low for annual rebalancing.

## $10k feasibility
Cleanly feasible. $2,500 each into VTI, TLT, GLD, SHY (or BIL). Fractional
shares available at most brokers.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Buy $2,500 each: VTI, TLT, GLD, SHY (or BIL for shorter cash).
2. Enable DRIP on all four.
3. Set January annual rebalance reminder.
4. If account is taxable, prefer holding GLD inside IRA to avoid the 28%
   collectibles rate.
