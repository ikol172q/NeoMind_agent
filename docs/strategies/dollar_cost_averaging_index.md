# Dollar-Cost Averaging into a Total-Market Index

## What it is
Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) is the practice of investing a fixed dollar amount
on a regular schedule (weekly, biweekly, or monthly) into a broad index ETF
such as VTI (Vanguard Total Stock Market) or VOO (S&P 500). You buy more shares
when prices are lower and fewer when they are higher, smoothing out the average
cost basis. Over decades the strategy compounds via market drift plus dividend
reinvestment. It is the default recommendation of the Bogleheads community and
Vanguard's own investor education materials.

## When it works
DCA works in any market that trends upward over the long run. Since 1928 the
S&P 500 has produced positive returns in approximately 70% of rolling 12-month
windows and ~94% of rolling 20-year windows. The strategy especially benefits
new investors who fear deploying a lump sum at "the top" — DCA defers
psychological regret by spreading entries.

## When it fails
- **Permanently impaired markets**: Japan's Nikkei DCA from 1989 onward was
  flat for 30 years. DCA does not save you if the index itself never recovers.
- **Lump-sum statistical superiority**: Vanguard's research shows lump-sum
  investing beats DCA ~2/3 of the time on a pure-return basis. DCA pays a small
  expected-return tax for psychological comfort.
- **Behavioral failure**: many investors stop contributing during deep
  drawdowns (2008, March 2020) — exactly when DCA produces its best buys.
- **Inflation drag**: real returns can be negative in stagflation regimes
  (1973-1981).

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Long-term capital-gains rate applies after 12-month hold per lot.
- Reinvested dividends create new tax lots — track basis carefully via your
  broker's specific-lot accounting.
- Wash-sale risk is low if you simply buy and hold; it appears only if you
  sell at a loss and re-buy within 30 days.
- IRS Publication 550 covers cost basis and dividend treatment.

## $10k feasibility
Highly feasible. Open an account at Fidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard. All three
offer fractional shares of VTI/VOO with $0 commission. $10k can be deployed
all at once or laddered over 4-12 weeks.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Open a brokerage account (Fidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard).
2. Link a checking account.
3. Set up an automatic recurring purchase: $200/week of VTI (or whatever
   amount fits your savings rate).
4. Enable dividend reinvestment (DRIP).
5. Bookmark the account, then close the tab. Do not check daily.
