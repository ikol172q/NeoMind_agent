# Dividend-Growth ETF (SCHD / VIG / DGRO)

## What it is
A dividend-growth ETF holds a basket of US companies that have a track record
of raising their dividends consistently (typically 5-10+ years). Three popular
choices: Schwab's SCHD (Dow Jones US Dividend 100), Vanguard's VIG (S&P US
Dividend Growers), and iShares's DGRO. The thesis is that companies which
successfully raise dividends over decades tend to be financially disciplined,
profitable, and resilient — which historically correlates with above-average
risk-adjusted returns.

## When it works
- **Income generation**: 3-4% yield from SCHD is steady through most regimes.
- **Defensive in late-cycle**: dividend growers tend to underperform less in
  bear markets (2022 saw SCHD outperform SPY).
- **Tax-efficient growth**: most distributions are qualified dividends, taxed
  at LTCG rates if you meet the holding-period rule.

## When it fails
- **Tech-led rallies**: SCHD heavily underweights tech and underperformed badly
  in 2020-2021 and again 2023-2024 vs S&P 500.
- **Dividend traps**: companies with collapsing fundamentals can show high
  yields (regional banks 2023, real-estate REITs 2022).
- **Sector concentration**: heavy in financials, staples, energy — interest-rate
  sensitive.
- **Dividend cuts**: dividend-growth status can be revoked (GE 2018, Wells
  Fargo 2020). Index ejects laggards but only after the cut.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Qualified dividends are taxed at 0%/15%/20% LTCG rates IF the holding-period
  rule is met (held at least 61 days during the 121-day window around ex-date,
  per IRS Publication 550).
- DRIP creates many small tax lots; this matters if you ever harvest losses.
- Wash-sale risk is low for buy-and-hold; rises if you tax-loss harvest.

## $10k feasibility
Single-ticker exposure works at any size. $1,000-2,000 is a sensible starter
allocation; build over time with new contributions. Fractional shares allow
exact-dollar buys.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Decide which dividend-growth ETF (SCHD has lowest expense ratio at 0.06%).
2. Buy $1,000-2,000 this week.
3. Enable DRIP for compounding.
4. Set a quarterly reminder to add new contributions.
5. Review the index methodology once per year — read what got added/dropped.
