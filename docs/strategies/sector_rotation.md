# Sector Rotation (Business-Cycle)

## What it is
Sector rotation is a tactical equity strategy that overweights sectors expected
to lead in the current phase of the business cycle. Fidelity's framework maps
the cycle into early / mid / late / recession, with leaders in each phase:
- Early: financials, consumer discretionary, industrials
- Mid: technology, communication services
- Late: energy, materials, healthcare
- Recession: staples, utilities, healthcare

Implemented via SPDR sector ETFs (XLK, XLE, XLF, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLV, XLI, XLB,
XLRE, XLC). Allocation is typically held 3-9 months and reassessed monthly.

## When it works
- **Clear regime transitions**: 2020 reopening (cyclicals lead), 2022 rate
  shock (energy and value lead).
- **Macro indicators align**: yield curve, PMI, CPI, unemployment trending
  consistently.
- **Discretionary overlay** beats systematic factor models when fundamentals
  are clear.

## When it fails
- **Mid-cycle transitions are hard to date**: NBER labels recessions after
  the fact. Real-time the signals are noisy.
- **Whipsaws**: 2022 had multiple regime fakeouts. Energy led, then collapsed
  in late 2023.
- **Tech-led secular trends override the cycle**: 2023 AI rally was
  cycle-agnostic.
- **Tax friction**: holding sectors 3-6 months means short-term gains.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Most positions held <12 months → ordinary-income tax rates.
- Wash-sale risk if you exit XLK and re-enter within 30 days. SPDR sector
  ETFs are not "substantially identical" to other sector ETFs in most
  interpretations, so swapping XLK for VGT or FTEC is generally safe — but
  consult a tax pro.
- IRS Pub 550 governs holding-period rules.

## $10k feasibility
Feasible at $10k. Run with 2-3 ETFs concurrently of $3,000-5,000 each.
Avoids PDT trouble if you hold positions for ≥1 month.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Read Fidelity's business-cycle update (linked below).
2. Identify the current phase (early/mid/late/recession).
3. Pick 2 sectors expected to lead. Allocate 70% across them, 30% cash/SHY.
4. Set a monthly calendar review — adjust if phase changes.
5. Use a simple log: date, allocation, reason. Review at year-end.
