# Small-Cap Value (AVUV / VIOV / IJS)

## What it is
Small-cap value combines two academically-documented factors: size (small
companies historically outperform large) and value (cheap-on-fundamentals
stocks outperform expensive). The Fama-French model identifies SCV as one of
the strongest historical premia. Implementations include Avantis AVUV
(actively managed factor exposure), Vanguard VIOV (S&P 600 SmallCap Value),
and iShares IJS. Long-run premium ~3-4%/yr historically over total market,
with substantially higher volatility.

## When it works
- **Post-recession recovery rallies**: 2009-2010, 2020 H2 — SCV fly.
- **Reflationary regimes**: 2022 oil/inflation rebound saw SCV outperform.
- **Long horizons**: 10+ year holds historically capture the premium.

## When it fails
- **Decade-long underperformance**: 2010-2020 SCV lost to S&P 500 by ~3%/yr.
- **Higher volatility**: drawdowns can hit 50%+. Many investors quit.
- **Small-cap "junk" component**: small-cap value indexes can include
  unprofitable companies; AVUV's profitability screen partially addresses
  this.
- **Liquidity thinner**: bid/ask wider than mega-cap funds.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Standard equity ETF treatment. Hold >12 months for LTCG.
- Most distributions qualified dividends → LTCG rates if holding-period rule
  met (IRS Pub 550).
- Wash-sale: AVUV vs VBR vs IJS may not be substantially identical (different
  index methodologies) — generally swappable for tax-loss harvesting, but
  consult a tax pro.

## $10k feasibility
Trivial. Allocate 10-20% of $10k as a satellite tilt ($1,000-2,000).

## First-week starter checklist
1. Pick a vehicle: AVUV (active, ~0.25% ER), VIOV (passive, ~0.10%), IJS
   (passive, ~0.18%). AVUV's profitability screen has historically improved
   the SCV premium capture.
2. Buy $1,000-2,000 alongside core (VTI or VOO).
3. Commit to a 5-10 year evaluation horizon.
4. Avoid frequent trading — premium realization requires patience.
