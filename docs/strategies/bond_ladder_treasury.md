# Treasury Bond Ladder

## What it is
A bond ladder is a series of US Treasury holdings with staggered maturities
(e.g., 6 months, 1 yr, 2 yr, 3 yr, 5 yr). As each "rung" matures, you reinvest
into a new long rung, maintaining the ladder. This converts the Treasury market
into a steady stream of returning principal plus interest, while smoothing
interest-rate risk: short rungs reprice quickly, long rungs lock in current
rates. Built directly via TreasuryDirect.gov or via your broker's secondary
market.

## When it works
- **Rising-rate regimes**: short rungs reinvest at higher yields (2022-2024).
- **Predictable cash needs**: each rung's maturity provides a known cash date.
- **Capital preservation**: held to maturity, you receive face value (USTs
  have no credit risk).
- **State-tax exemption**: Treasury interest is NOT subject to state or local
  income tax (federal only) — meaningful in CA, NY, NJ, etc.

## When it fails
- **Falling-rate regimes**: rolled-over rungs reinvest at lower yields,
  compressing income.
- **Real-return failure**: 2021-2022 saw nominal positive but deeply negative
  real returns as inflation outpaced yields.
- **Liquidity drag if sold early**: pre-maturity sale can realize MTM losses.
  TLT held in 2022 lost 31%; individual long bonds saw similar damage.
- **Opportunity cost in equity bull markets**: stable but unspectacular.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Treasury interest: **federal taxable, state and local exempt** (26 U.S.C.
  §3124, IRS Pub 550).
- T-bills (≤52 weeks): zero-coupon, gain treated as interest at maturity.
- T-notes/bonds (≥2 years): semiannual coupon payments, taxed annually as
  ordinary interest.
- No qualified-dividend treatment — never LTCG-eligible on the interest.
- Best held in taxable account if you live in a high-state-tax state, since
  IRA already shelters everything.

## $10k feasibility
Highly feasible. TreasuryDirect minimum is $100 per security; brokers like
Fidelity and Schwab offer secondary-market T-bills with no commission. A
5-rung ladder of $2,000 each fits cleanly.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Open a TreasuryDirect.gov account (or use your existing broker).
2. Buy $2,000 each at auction or secondary: 13-week, 26-week, 1-year,
   2-year, 3-year.
3. As each rung matures, roll into the longest rung (3-yr or 5-yr) to
   maintain the ladder.
4. Track interest received for tax filing — Form 1099-INT or TreasuryDirect's
   1099 download.
