# International Developed Markets ETF (VXUS / IEFA)

## What it is
A single-ticker exposure to developed-market equities outside the US:
Europe, Japan, UK, Australia, Canada, plus emerging markets in some
implementations. Vanguard VXUS holds ~8,500 non-US stocks (developed +
emerging) at ~0.05% ER. iShares IEFA tracks developed markets ex-US ex-Canada.
The role: complete the global equity portfolio. The US is only ~60% of global
market capitalization; holding only US is a deliberate concentration bet.

## When it works
- **US-underperformance regimes**: 2000-2007 (US was decade flat, EAFE soared).
- **Currency tailwinds**: weaker USD boosts unhedged international returns.
- **Rebalancing dividend**: when international lags, rebalancing systematically
  buys low and sells US high.

## When it fails
- **US-led decades**: 2010-2022 saw international lag the S&P 500 by 3-4%/yr.
  This is not a short period — 12 years.
- **Currency drag**: strong USD (2014-2016) hurt unhedged international.
- **Geopolitical concentration**: VXUS has heavy Japan + EU exposure.
- **Investor capitulation**: many sold international after the 2010s lost
  decade and missed any subsequent rotation.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- VXUS distributes foreign-source dividends. Foreign-tax-paid passes through
  to your 1099-DIV; claim as **Foreign Tax Credit on Form 1116** (or take the
  itemized deduction, but FTC is usually better).
- **Critical**: hold international in a TAXABLE account, NOT an IRA — the FTC
  cannot be claimed against tax-deferred IRA income.
- Hold >12 months for LTCG. Most dividends are qualified.
- IRS Form 1116 instructions cover the FTC mechanics.

## $10k feasibility
Trivial. $1,000-3,000 as the international leg of a global portfolio.
Fractional shares supported.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Pick: VXUS (developed + EM, ~0.05% ER) or IEFA (developed-only, ~0.07%).
   VXUS is more complete; pair with VWO for separate EM if desired.
2. Buy $2,000-3,000 in your TAXABLE account (not IRA).
3. Enable DRIP.
4. At year-end, look at your 1099-DIV "Foreign tax paid" box; claim FTC on
   Form 1116 with your tax return.
