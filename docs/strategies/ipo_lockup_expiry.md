# IPO Lockup-Expiration Short Bias

## What it is
After an IPO, insiders (founders, employees, pre-IPO investors) are typically
restricted from selling for 90-180 days. When the lockup expires, a wave of
insider selling can pressure the stock price. The trade: identify upcoming
lockup expirations of stocks still trading above their IPO price, with large
unlock share counts relative to current float, and structure a defined-risk
bearish position to expire 30-45 days post-lockup. Per user constraint, this
catalog uses ONLY put debit spreads — NEVER naked shorts or naked puts.

## When it works
- **Heavy insider concentration**: pre-IPO PE/VC funds with mandate to
  distribute holdings.
- **Stock above IPO price**: insiders have profit incentive to sell.
- **Concentrated unlock**: when most of share count unlocks at once.

## When it fails
- **Insiders hold**: Snowflake's 2021 lockup saw minimal insider selling —
  the trade lost.
- **Strong fundamentals override**: AI-cycle names (NVDA, Palantir) shrugged
  off lockup waves.
- **Edge has compressed**: well-known trade since the dot-com era.
- **Constructive-sale rules**: hedging long positions can trigger §1259
  constructive-sale tax events. Read IRS rules.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Defined-risk put spread held <30-60 days → short-term capital loss/gain,
  ordinary rates.
- Equity options: NOT §1256.
- IRS Pub 550 §1092 covers straddle/offsetting-position rules — wash-sale
  and constructive-sale interactions are complex. CONSULT A TAX PRO.
- Per user rule: NEVER use naked options. The constructive-sale rules
  primarily affect those hedging long positions, but defined-risk put
  spreads are usually clean.

## $10k feasibility
Feasible. Allocate ~$500-1,000 max per setup (the full debit on a put
spread). Run 1-2 setups per quarter at most.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Track IPO calendar at NASDAQ or SEC EDGAR.
2. 30 days before lockup, check: stock above IPO price? Large unlock? Heavy
   pre-IPO insider holdings (read the S-1)?
3. If criteria align, buy a 30-45 DTE OTM put debit spread (e.g., $5-wide,
   ~$1.50 debit). Max risk = debit.
4. Exit at 50-100% of max profit, OR at expiry, whichever comes first.
5. NEVER add to losing trades. NEVER use naked structures.
