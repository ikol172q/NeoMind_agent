# Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD)

## What it is
PEAD is one of the most-documented anomalies in academic finance: stocks that
beat earnings by a wide margin (high SUE — Standardized Unexpected Earnings)
continue to outperform for the following 60-90 days. The market underreacts to
earnings surprises and the price drifts in the direction of the surprise.
Retail implementation: screen for stocks that gap up >5% on earnings release
with both EPS beat AND revenue beat AND raised forward guidance, buy at the
close of report day, hold 60 days, hard stop at -10%.

## When it works
- **Concentrated leadership periods**: 2023 mega-cap tech earnings → multi-
  quarter drifts.
- **Wide SUE**: top decile of surprises produces the bulk of the premium.
- **Mid-cap names with thin coverage**: less institutional reaction speed,
  more drift.

## When it fails
- **Premium has compressed** post-2010 as institutions adopted the strategy.
- **Single-stock idiosyncratic risk**: one bad next-quarter print can erase
  a quarter of returns.
- **Macro overrides**: 2022 saw beats sold; the macro regime mattered more
  than earnings.
- **PDT-relevant if you actively manage exits** — though 60-day hold helps.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- 60-day hold → all gains short-term, ordinary-income rates.
- Wash-sale risk if you exit at a loss and re-enter the next earnings cycle
  within 30 days (IRS Pub 550).
- Earnings drift on small-caps may include companies with NOLs, special tax
  situations — read the 10-Q.

## $10k feasibility
Feasible. Concentrated portfolio of 5-8 names per quarter, $1,000-2,000 each.
Cash buffer 10-20%. Discipline on the -10% stop is non-negotiable.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Subscribe to an earnings-calendar source (Yahoo Finance, Earnings Whispers).
2. Wait for the first earnings cycle starting (typically mid-month after a
   quarter-end).
3. Build a screen: stocks that gap up >5% on report day AND beat EPS AND
   beat revenue AND raised forward guidance.
4. Buy at the close of report day. Set stop at -10% from entry.
5. Hold 60 days or until stopped. Log every trade.
