# MCHI Buy-and-Hold (Broad China)

## What it is
iShares MSCI China ETF (MCHI) provides broad exposure to large- and mid-cap
Chinese equities — the index covers ~85% of the China equity universe across
Hong Kong-listed stocks, ADRs, and selected A-shares. Allocation as a small
satellite (5-15%) within a global portfolio offers exposure to a market that
trades at deep valuation discounts to US equities and may benefit from
eventual mean reversion. This is a long-term value/diversification bet, not
a tactical trade.

## When it works
- **Mean reversion from extreme discounts**: P/E ratios hit single digits
  in 2022, suggesting potential.
- **Diversification benefits** in periods of US-equity drawdowns (2000-2007).
- **Currency reserves rebalancing**: structural USD-down, EM-up regime.

## When it fails
- **Lost decade is historical fact**: MCHI is roughly flat 2010-2024 in USD
  terms.
- **Single-name concentration**: top 10 holdings (Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan)
  are >35% of fund.
- **Geopolitical risk**: Taiwan tensions, sanctions, capital controls.
- **VIE structural risk** for ADR-component holdings.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Foreign-source dividends pass through; **claim Foreign Tax Credit on Form
  1116** in your taxable account.
- Hold in TAXABLE account, not IRA — FTC is unusable in IRA.
- Hold >12 months for LTCG.
- Most distributions qualified dividends (IRS Pub 550 — verify each year
  on 1099-DIV).
- Wash-sale: MCHI vs FXI may not be substantially identical (different
  index methodologies); generally swappable for tax-loss harvesting.

## $10k feasibility
Trivial. Allocate 5-15% of $10k ($500-1,500) as a satellite. Single ticker,
fractional shares supported.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Buy $500-1,500 MCHI in your TAXABLE account.
2. Enable DRIP.
3. Plan a 5+ year evaluation horizon. Add via DCA if conviction grows.
4. Don't add more than 15% of total portfolio to China exposure
   (concentration risk).
5. At year-end, claim FTC on Form 1116 with your tax filing.
