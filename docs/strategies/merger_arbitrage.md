# Merger Arbitrage (Cash Deals)

## What it is
Merger arbitrage (or "risk arbitrage") captures the spread between a target
company's market price and the announced acquisition price. After a deal is
announced, the target typically trades 1-5% below the deal price, reflecting
deal-completion risk and time-value-of-money. If the deal closes, you collect
the spread; if it breaks, you take a substantial loss as the target reverts
to pre-deal levels. Cash deals (vs stock-for-stock) simplify the trade by
eliminating ratio risk.

## When it works
- **Friendly deals with regulatory clarity**: most close in 4-8 months.
- **Strategic vs financial buyers**: strategic buyers usually walk through
  antitrust unless market share is concentrated.
- **High-rate environments**: spreads widen, providing better annualized
  returns.

## When it fails
- **Antitrust breaks**: NVIDIA-ARM (2022), Adobe-Figma (2023). Targets fell
  20-40% on announcement.
- **Single-deal concentration**: pros run baskets of 20+ deals; one break
  doesn't ruin the year. Retail concentration in 1-2 deals is genuinely
  risky.
- **Spread compression**: in low-rate environments, annualized spreads can
  drop to 4-5% — barely above T-bill yields.
- **Funding-failure breaks**: 2022 saw multiple PE deals collapse on
  financing.

## Tax & compliance considerations
- Cash-merger closes are treated as a **sale on closing date** at the cash
  price received. Gain/loss = cash received minus your basis.
- Most spreads close <12 months → short-term capital gains, ordinary-income
  rates.
- Special rule: if the deal includes stock + cash, the stock portion may
  qualify for tax-deferred treatment under §368 (consult a tax pro and the
  proxy/S-4 deal documents).
- Wash-sale risk low — once the deal closes, the company's gone.

## $10k feasibility
Feasible but concentrated. $10k is enough for one 100-share position in a
typical $20-40 stock target. Run only deals with high regulatory clarity and
strategic buyers. NEVER allocate more than 30% of $10k to a single deal.

## First-week starter checklist
1. Subscribe to a deals tracker (e.g., MergerArbitrageMonitor, BAML reports
   if accessible).
2. Pick ONE announced cash deal with: friendly board, no major antitrust
   concerns, expected close < 6 months.
3. Buy target at ~1-2% below deal price. Position $2,000-3,000 max.
4. Track key dates: shareholder vote, regulatory deadlines, expected close.
5. Hold to close. Do not panic-sell on rumor; do panic-sell on actual
   antitrust filing of substance.
